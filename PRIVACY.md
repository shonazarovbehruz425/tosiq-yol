# Privacy Policy — Wrong Way (To'siq Yo'l)

**Last updated:** 12 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the **Wrong Way** Telegram bot ([@WrongWayGameBot](https://t.me/WrongWayGameBot)) and its Mini App (the "Service") collect, use, and protect your information. By starting the bot or opening the Mini App, you agree to the practices described here.

The Service is an online 1v1 strategy board game. We collect the minimum data needed to run the game, matchmaking, leaderboards, and the friends system.

---

## 1. Who we are

- **Service:** Wrong Way (To'siq Yo'l) — a Telegram bot and Mini App game.
- **Bot:** @WrongWayGameBot
- **Contact:** [@WrongWaySupportBot](https://t.me/WrongWaySupportBot)

We are an independent game project, not affiliated with Telegram.

---

## 2. Information we collect

We only create an account for you when you press **Start (/start)** in the bot. Simply opening the Mini App without starting the bot does **not** create a stored account.

When you use the Service, we may store:

- **Telegram account basics** provided by Telegram: your numeric Telegram user ID, first name, and username (if you have one).
- **Language preference** — the language you select in the bot or app.
- **Display name** — an optional username you set in Settings for the leaderboard.
- **Approximate country** — derived from your IP address at connection time using a third‑party IP‑geolocation lookup. We store only the resulting **country** (e.g. "Uzbekistan"); we do **not** store your full IP address.
- **Game data** — match results, wins, losses, draws, rating, move history of games, board settings, and timestamps.
- **Friends data** — your friends list and pending friend requests within the Service.
- **In‑game messages** — chat messages and reactions sent during an online match are relayed to your opponent in real time. We do not build long‑term archives of in‑game chat.

We do **not** collect your phone number, email, contacts, precise GPS location, or payment information.

---

## 3. How we use your information

We use the information to:

- Create and maintain your game profile.
- Match you with opponents and run online, friend, and bot games.
- Maintain leaderboards and game statistics.
- Power the friends system (requests, invites, online status).
- Show your approximate country and online status to others where relevant (e.g. friends, leaderboard).
- Send you messages through the bot related to the Service (including occasional announcements from the administrators).
- Operate, maintain, debug, and improve the Service.

We do **not** sell your personal data or use it for third‑party advertising.

## 4. Legal basis

We process your data to provide a service you requested (performing the game features you use) and based on our legitimate interest in operating and improving the Service.

## 5. Data sharing and disclosure

- **Other players:** your display name, approximate country, online status, and game results may be visible to other players (e.g. on the leaderboard, in the friends list, and during matches).
- **Telegram:** the Service runs on Telegram's platform; your use is also subject to [Telegram's Privacy Policy](https://telegram.org/privacy).
- **Service providers:** we use hosting infrastructure and a third‑party IP‑geolocation lookup to determine country. These providers process data only to deliver their function.
- **Database backup:** the game database may be backed up to a private Telegram channel controlled by the administrators for persistence and disaster recovery. Access is restricted to the administrators.
- **Legal:** we may disclose information if required by law or to protect the rights, safety, or security of users and the Service.

## 6. Data storage and security

- Data is stored on our hosting provider and in an administrator‑controlled private backup.
- Access to the admin dashboard is protected by authentication and is restricted to authorized administrators.
- We apply reasonable technical measures to protect your data. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
